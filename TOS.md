## 2. Description of Service

Study Room is a mobile application that enables users to:

- **Record timelapse study sessions** using the device's front-facing camera.
- **Share timelapse videos** with small, private accountability groups called "Rooms."
- **Track study statistics** including session duration, streaks, and total study time.
- **React to sessions** posted by other Room members using emoji reactions.
- **Create and join Rooms** — small groups of 3 to 25 people who know each other for mutual study accountability.

Study Room is designed for small-group accountability between people who know each other. It is **not** a public social media platform. Content shared within Rooms is only visible to verified members of those Rooms.

## 6. Camera Usage and Timelapse Recording

### 6.1 Camera Access

The App requires access to your device's front-facing (selfie) camera to capture timelapse frames during study sessions. Key facts about camera usage:

- The camera is **only active during a study session** that you explicitly start.
- The camera is **never accessed** when you are not in an active session.
- Image frames are captured approximately every **10 seconds** during an active session.
- Frames are stored **locally on your device** during the session.
- The App does **not** record audio. Only still image frames are captured.
- You may revoke camera permissions at any time via your device settings, though this will prevent you from recording new study sessions.

### 6.2 On-Device Processing

Timelapse videos are stitched together from captured frames **entirely on your device**. No raw frames are uploaded to our servers. Only the final timelapse video (after your review and approval) is uploaded if you choose to share it with a Room.

### 6.3 Your Responsibility

You are solely responsible for:

- The content captured in your timelapse recordings.
- Ensuring your study environment is appropriate for recording (e.g., no sensitive documents, private information, or other people who have not consented visible in frame).
- Complying with any applicable laws regarding recording in your jurisdiction.
